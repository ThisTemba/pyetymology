==Spanish==

===Etymology===
From {{inh|es|la|plicāre}}, present active infinitive of {{m|la|plicō||I fold}}, ultimately from {{der|es|ine-pro|*pleḱ-||to plait, to weave}}. The semantic shift over time from "to fold" is also found in some other Romance cognates, and may be linked to the idea of folding sails when arriving at a port, especially in Iberian Romance where naval tradition was strong. Compare {{cog|pt|chegar}}; however cf. also {{cog|ro|pleca||to leave}}, with the opposite meaning, possibly because there the word was associated with folding up tents and leaving. See also the Spanish doublet {{doublet|es|plegar|t1=to fold|notext=1}}. Another theory instead derives ''llegar'' from a {{inh|es|VL.|plicāre}} as a regressive derivation from Classical Latin {{m|la|applicāre||apply, add, attach, join to}}<ref>https://www.scribd.com/document/158436236/Diccionario-Critico-Etimologico-castellano-G-MA-Corominas-Joan-pdf</ref>. Cognate with {{cog|en|ply}}.
